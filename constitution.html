Preamble

We, the members of the Lawrence Community Advocacy Coalition (LCAC), united by a common purpose to promote environmental awareness, social justice, and educational equity, establish this Constitution to guide the operations, conduct, and mission of our club.

Article I: Name and Purpose

The official name of this club shall be the Lawrence Community Advocacy Coalition (LCAC).

LCAC provides a structured, youth-inclusive forum for community-driven advocacy focused on environmental protection, social equity, and accessible education.

Article II: Membership

  • Membership is open to all students and community members who support LCAC’s mission.
  • All members may attend meetings, propose ideas, and participate in events.
  • Only members aged 16 and older may serve in leadership roles or vote in club elections.

Article III: Executive Board Structure

The Executive Board shall oversee LCAC’s activities and decision-making. It consists of the following roles:

Voting Members

  • Chairperson – Leads the club, facilitates meetings, and sets agendas.
  • 8 Representatives – Serve as voting members, representing the interests of LCAC members.

Non-Voting Members

  • Treasurer – Manages club funds and budgeting.
  • Event Coordinator – Organizes club events and advocacy campaigns.
  • Outreach Coordinators (Unlimited) – Promote LCAC activities and engage with the community.
  • Advisors (2) – Provide guidance and support. If a voting position becomes vacant, an Advisor may temporarily fill the role until a new Representative is elected.

Article IV: Elections and Terms

  • All Executive Board positions are elected annually by voting-eligible members.
  • Elections shall be held at the end of each academic year.
  • Vacancies may be filled through special elections as needed.

Article V: Meetings and Procedures

  • LCAC shall hold meetings once a month, open to all members.
  • Meetings shall follow a structured agenda to ensure productivity.
  • Quorum Requirement: A meeting cannot proceed with official votes or decisions if more than two voting members are absent.
  • Decisions shall be made by majority agreement among voting members.

Article VI: Code of Conduct

  • Members are expected to foster a welcoming and respectful environment.
  • Disruptive or harmful behavior may result in mediation or removal from leadership roles, as determined by the Executive Board.

Article VII: Amendments

  • Any member aged 16 or older may propose amendments.
  • Proposed amendments must be submitted in writing and approved by two-thirds of the Executive Board.
  • Amendments shall be made public to all members upon ratification.

Article VIII: Ratification & Review

This Constitution shall go into effect upon approval by a majority of the initial Executive Board members.

The Constitution shall be reviewed every four years to ensure alignment with LCAC’s evolving mission and goals.
